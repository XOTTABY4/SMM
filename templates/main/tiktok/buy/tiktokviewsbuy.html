What Are TikTok Reels?

TikTok’s relatively new feature allows users to create short videos of up to 60 seconds with many options to add music, filters, and effects, which can be shared to users’ stories, explore feeds, and on the new Reels tab on the user’s profile.

TikTok Reels are similar to the short video platform TikTok and are available in more than 150 countries worldwide. Like TikTok, TikTok Reels have an explore page, where TikTok show reels from who you already follow, from influencers, from your geolocation, and especially which content you interact with. And sometimes content with the FEATURED label to help you discover original content.

How to Create TikTok Reels?

There are two options for creating TikTok Reels

  1. Click on the reels icon at the bottom of the TikTok app.
  2. Click on plus sign on “Your Story,” then click on the setting icon and select Reels.

After recording your reels video or uploading it from the gallery, you can use the built-in video editor of TikTok to add effects, music, slow motion effects, and various other options.

What Counts As A View On IG Reels?

On TikTok Reels, a view is only counted if the video has been watched for at least 3 seconds. It means that if someone scrolls, finds a reel, and watches it for less than 3 seconds, it won't count as a view. However, if someone watches the Reel for 3 seconds or more, it will count as a view and be included in the view count. TikTok Reels also track views from individual accounts, meaning it will only count as one view if the same person watches the same reel multiple times.
